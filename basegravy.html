My Base Gravy Well Worth The Effort!
So, This is all you will need, plus about a dozen onions, tennis ball size roughly chopped, and a tin of chopped tomatoes plus some spices. - The mix powder mentioned is just your own curry powder and can be made up of almost any spices that you favour. I would suggest use 4 heaped teaspoons of a mild curry powder, 3 of paprika (not smoked). 3 of turmeric, 3 of coriander powder, 2 of cumin powder and one of garam massala. Shake them all together with a lid on then pop them into a clean dry jar with a lid. There you have your own 'mix powder'

The white vegetable shown is a bit of mooli, which you can buy in any Asian shop and even find in supermarkets now. (It's a mild vegetable a bit like radish)

To start with, find a small glass bowl and add in a teaspoon of your spice mix powder, 1 of coriander, 1 of cumin, 1 of turmeric and maybe a little cinnamon and garam massala. Stir in enough sunflower oil to make a paste and leave to one side. Then top and tail and peel about a dozen tennis ball sized onions,and roughly chop them.

You will need of course a huge saucepan. I use my old pressure cooker saucepan which is just about big enough. Add about 350ml sunflower oil and heat, then add a tablespoon of garlic and ginger paste plus a squirt of tomato paste, stir briefly, when colour changes take off the heat and add a litre of water. Back on the heat and when up to a simmer, add the onions plus a teaspoon of salt. Stir to coat the onions with the oil/water

Then off to peel & chop the potato (1), carrot (1), green pepper and bit of cabbage (just a small amount) plus a piece of mooli.

Stir the onions again and add enough water to just cover. Add half a teaspoon chilli powder, and a teaspoon of turmeric. Stir some more and add the chopped veggies. Stir once more. Pop the lid on and notice the time. Boil gently for an hour. Allow to cool overnight if necessary, and then add a tin of chopped tomatoes before blending the mixture with a stick blender. Now add the spices mixed earlier and stir again. At this point, some like to add say half a can of evaporated milk, or some coconut, either from a block or powder form (not desicated!).It's all a matter of what turns you on! Back on the heat and simmer for another 30 minutes or so, adding more water if the consistency is too thick. Set aside the quantity you want to freeze. When you come to use the frozen portions you should defrost and add some water and bring up to a simmer before using. The gravy should be quite 'wet' almost like milk as the cooking in the pan later evaporates the water leaving the consistency you are looking for at that time. Usually the oil begins to show towards the end of cooking, then you know you are there!




Good Luck!

Now You Need To Know How To Cook Your Rice



For what it is worth, when buying some rice at our local Asian store, the lovely wife of the owner told me after washing the rice in warm water using my hand, rinse with cold water several times, then soak the rice 30 minutes or so. Get a pan of water boiling, and add a little oil and some salt. Drain the washed rice in a sieve and add it to the boiling water for 6/7 minutes. Then tip it back into the sieve to drain. Pour a kettle of cold water over the sieve and after it has drained, empty it into an oven proof container. Cover tightly and keep in the oven till ready to serve. Fluffing it up after about 5 minutes and dotting some colouring on opposite sides of the rice for later fluffing up again.

I sometimes use a small herb container thingy called a spice ball - and dangle that in the water from the outset, before adding my rice. In it I put about an inch of casia bark, a bay leaf (Asian) a couple of cloves, a couple of cardamom pods (slit) and maybe some black peppercorns. Also I add about half a teaspoon of yellow colouring powder to the water, or even a bit less just to take the whiteness off the rice.

If the mood takes me, I might chuck in a lump of lemon rind, or some jeera seeds.

After so many failures, my rice is now perfect every time. I took the lady a small bunch of flowers the next week and the owner said he was beginning to worry. I was the third man that week that had given his wife flowers!

By the way - I always cook my rice first before starting on my curries, then microwave before serving.
